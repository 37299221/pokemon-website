Effort Values & Individual Values

What are they?

EVs and IVs, although having a similar name, are two different things. They both do affect stats however.

EVs

Short for ’Effort Values’, EVs are used to increase the stat points slowly, which stop at an EV value of 252 per stat and 512 total. Depending on the pokemon's level, the increase in stats may not even be noticeable.

EVs are obtained by fainting other pokemon. However, it is not as simple as gaining experience. Each pokemon has a stat (HP, Atk, Sp.Atk, so on) that EVs are distributed on after being defeated. For example:

If a pokemon defeats a Pikachu (2 speed EVs) that pokemon will gain EVs in its speed stat. If someone defeats 1,000,000 Pikachus, the pokemon will have 252 EVs. this is because it cannot any more EVs, as it has reached its maximum EVs in speed.

In addition to this, EVs can even be gained by using items like carbos, HP ups, irons, etc.

IVs

Each pokemon when generated in a game will have a random number of IVs per stat, ranging from 0 to 31. These have an even more minimal effect on a pokemon stat than EVs do, as the majority of the time the effect of IVs can only be seen at level 100. Generally however, they do not have much of an effect on the main story of any Pokemon game and is only really useful in competitive pokemon battles.
