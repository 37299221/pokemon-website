Pokemon Moves

Introduction

The main way that pokemon battle each other is by using moves. Each move has a certain power, accuracy and power point (PP) value. In addition to this, they all have a type associated with it. Not all moves do damage however, and may do something completely different.

Attacking moves

Moves can be assigned to ‘Special’ or ‘Physical’, which determines what stat is used for damage calculation ingame.

Special = Sp.Atk

Physical = Atk

However, some moves like ‘counter’ have a varying power value depending on what happens in battle. ‘Counter’ in this example will only do damage if the pokemon using it has been hit with a physical attack

Status moves

Status moves have a variety of roles within a battle. Moves like ‘protect’ can negate the effects of an incoming attack and moves like swords dance’ can raise a stat. There are even more examples like this with even more effects, but are very helpful in battle.

Other important points

  • Critical hits- Critical hits are a luck-based event that only occurs on an attacking move (special or physical). Although changed in a few games, critical hits have a damage multiplier of 1.5. Critical hits can also completely nullify in-battle defense / special defense increases.
  • Priority - Although not many moves have this, some moves can ignore the speed stat. What I mean by this is if a move has +1 priority it will always move first. On the other hand, a -1 priority will cause it to move last. The only thing that can overcome this is either a larger priority (can be between -7 and +7).
  • Learning moves - The main ways a pokemon can learn moves is by levelling up or learning it via technical machines (which come in different varieties). However, there are other, more difficult ways that a pokemon can learn a move, these being:
    • Breeding for egg moves
    • Taught by move tutors
    • Evolving
    • Move reminders
