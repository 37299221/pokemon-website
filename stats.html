Pokemon Statistics

Pokemon statistics, or stats for short, are numbered values that are paired with each of the following six:

  • Attack (Atk)
  • Special Attack (Sp.atk)
  • Defense (Def)
  • Special Defense (Sp.def)
  • Speed
  • Health Points (HP)

What do stats do?

To put simply, the higher the number of a stat the more effective it is in battle. These stats are used to calculate a few things:

  • Amount of damage done / taken after a move has been used (Atk or Sp.atk / Def or Sp.def or HP)
  • If a pokemon uses its move before the other (Speed)
  • The amount of health healed after a healing move or item has been used. (HP)

What can affect these stats?

There are a number of things that can affect these stats.

The first thing that may change a pokemon's stat in a battle are stat increases/ decreases. These occur either by a move or an ability, such as swords dance (a move that raises attack by two stages) or huge power (an ability that doubles your atk stat).

Another thing that will affect stats in battle are status effects. The only status effects that affect stats in particular are burn and paralyze. Burn halves a pokemon's attack and paralyze halfs speed in addition to their main effects.

Some items may even change stats. These will need to be equipped in the bag outside of battle, however. Some examples may be:

  • Choice scarf (speed increase)
  • Eviolite (Defense and special defense increase)
  • Choice band (Attack increase)

In addition to this, there are smaller changes to stats that are different for every pokemon, even ones that are the exact same species. These ‘things’ that change stats are known as:

  • EVs (effort values)
  • IVs (individual values)
  • Natures

Natures are made to increase one stat and decrease another. These increases and decreases can even be cancelled out if one overlaps the other. In total, there are 25 different natures that pokemon can have, but each pokemon can only have one. A chart below shows what each nature is called and that stats are changed (blue means stat lowering and red means stat raising).

+ Def Bold Docile Impish Lax Relaxed
+ Sp. Atk Modest Mild Bashful Rash Quiet
+ Sp. Def Calm Gentle Careful Quirky Sassy
+ Speed Timid Hasty Jolly Naive Serious

As can be seen above, the only stat that cannot be increased is HP. Other than this, all stats can be changed by natures.
