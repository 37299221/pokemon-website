Pokemon Types

Introduction to types

In Pokemon, there are a total of 18 different types. All pokemon come with one or two of these types, which they will always have (a squirtle will always be water type). In addition to this, all moves come with one type (but not two!) These types give pokemon their weaknesses and strengths. Below shows an example of how strengths and weaknesses work:

Fire type attack

+ Water type pokemon

= Not very effective

Water type attack

+ Fire type pokemon

= Super effective

So what does super effective and not very effective mean?

The answer to this question is simple; not very effective attacks do less damage and super effective attacks do more. However, the amount of damage is based on a multiplier. These are as follows:

  • ‘Not very effective’ attacks have a multiplier of 0.5 (or 0.25 if the opposing pokemon has a double strength)
  • ‘Super effective’ attacks have a multiplier of 2.0 (or 4.0 if the opposing pokemon has a double weakness)

Immunities

Along with weaknesses and strengths, pokemon can also have immunities (shown in the type chart above as ‘ 0 ’). This simply means that pokemon that are attacked by a move that they are immune to will not take any damage.

An example of a common immunity seen in pokemon is held by the ghost type. Gastly, a ghost type, is immune to both normal and fighting types.Abilities can also make a pokemon immune to specific types.

Same Type Attack Bonus

Pokemon types do not only exist for the purpose of super effectiveness either. ‘STAB’, also known as Same Type Attack Bonus, increases the damage output of a move used. For example, if a squirtle were to use water gun, it would gain bonus damage to the opposing pokemon as squirtle is a water type pokemon and water gun is a water type move.
